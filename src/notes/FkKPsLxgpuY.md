### 智力测试的起源：斯皮尔曼的“g因子”

智力测试的理念可以追溯到几百年前，但第一个具体的突破发生在1904年。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">The idea of intelligence testing goes back hundreds of years, but the first concrete breakthrough occurred in 1904.</p>
</details>

英国心理学家**查尔斯·斯皮尔曼**（Charles Spearman: 英国心理学家，提出了智力双因素理论）当时正在研究学生在不同学科中的成绩，他想知道学生在一门学科（如英语）中的表现与在另一门学科（如数学）中的表现之间有何关联。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">English psychologist Charles Spearman was studying students' grades in different subjects and he wondered how their performance in one subject, like English, would relate to their performance in another, like Math.</p>
</details>

一种可能性是，学生数学成绩越好，英语成绩就越差，这可能是因为他们花更多时间在数学上，从而减少了学习英语的时间。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">One option would be that the better a student did in math, the worse they would do in English, maybe because they spent more time on their math work and so had less time to devote to English.</p>
</details>

在这种情况下，不同学科的表现将呈负相关。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">So performance in different subjects would be negatively correlated.</p>
</details>

另一种可能性是，一门学科的表现与另一门学科的表现完全无关。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Another option was that performance in one subject would be completely unrelated to performance in another.</p>
</details>

毕竟，不同学科需要不同的技能，所以分数可能完全不相关。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">After all, different subjects require different skill sets, so maybe marks would be totally uncorrelated.</p>
</details>

第三种可能性是，学生数学成绩越好，英语成绩也越好。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">The third option was that the better a student did in math, the better they would do in English.</p>
</details>

换句话说，他们的分数将呈正相关。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">In other words, their marks would be positively correlated.</p>
</details>

**相关系数**（correlation coefficient: 衡量两个变量之间线性关系强弱和方向的统计指标）可以在负一到正一之间变化。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">A correlation coefficient can vary anywhere from negative one to positive one.</p>
</details>

相关系数为负一表示完美的负相关，意味着一个变量的增加对应着另一个变量精确、可预测的减少。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">A correlation coefficient of negative one indicates a perfect negative correlation, meaning an increase in one variable corresponds to a precise, predictable decrease in the other variable.</p>
</details>

同样，相关系数为正一表示完美的正相关。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Similarly, a correlation of positive one indicates a perfect positive correlation.</p>
</details>

相关系数为零表示两个变量之间没有关系。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</p>
<p class="english-text">A correlation of zero indicates no relationship between the two variables.</p>
</details>

任何介于零和一之间的值表示正相关，但数据存在一些随机分散。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">And any value between zero and one indicates a positive correlation, but the data has some random spread.</p>
</details>

相关系数的平方表示一个变量中可以由另一个变量的变化解释的变异量。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">The square of the correlation coefficient tells you the amount of variation in one variable that can be explained by variation in the other variable.</p>
</details>

例如，如果相关系数为0.5，那么一个变量中25%的变异可以由另一个变量解释。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">For example, if the correlation coefficient is 0.5, then 25% of the variation in one variable can be explained by the other.</p>
</details>

当斯皮尔曼分析他的数据时，他发现了一个明显的正相关。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">When Spearman analyzed his data, he found a clear positive correlation.</p>
</details>

数学成绩好的学生在英语方面也往往表现更好，相关系数为0.64。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Students who did better in math also tended to do better in English, and the correlation coefficient was 0.64.</p>
</details>

但数学和英语并不是学生们学习的唯一科目。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">But Math and English weren't the only subjects the students studied.</p>
</details>

他们还学习了古典文学和法语。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">They also took Classics and French.</p>
</details>

当斯皮尔曼查看所有这些科目之间的相关性时，他发现了相同的模式。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">And when Spearman looked at the correlations between all of these subjects, he found the same pattern.</p>
</details>

在一门科目中表现好的学生，往往在所有科目中都表现出色。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Students who did well in one subject tended to do well in them all.</p>
</details>

那么，如何解释这一观察结果呢？

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">So how do you explain this observation?</p>
</details>

斯皮尔曼提出，每个人都具有某种程度的**一般智力**（general intelligence），他称之为“**g因子**”（g-factor: 查尔斯·斯皮尔曼提出的一种假设性心理结构，代表了所有认知任务中共享的基础智力能力）。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Well, Spearman proposed that each person has some level of general intelligence, what he called the g-factor.</p>
</details>

这个概念旨在捕捉学生学习新材料、识别模式和批判性思考的速度，而无论具体学科如何，这解释了为什么学生在不同科目中的分数是相关的。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">This construct was meant to capture how quickly students could learn new material, recognize patterns, and think critically regardless of the subject matter, which explains why students' scores across subjects are correlated.</p>
</details>

那些g因子高的人在所有科目中都得分高，而g因子低的人在所有科目中都得分差。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Those with high g, score well on all subjects and those with low g, score poorly on all subjects.</p>
</details>

斯皮尔曼将他的结论发表在一篇题为《客观确定和测量的一般智力》的论文中。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Spearman published his conclusions in a paper titled "General Intelligence" Objectively Determined and Measured.</p>
</details>

但相关性并非完美。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">But the correlations weren't perfect.</p>
</details>

因此，除了g因子，斯皮尔曼还提出了特定学科因素，即“**s因子**”（s-factors: 斯皮尔曼提出的特定智力因素，与g因子相对，代表了在特定任务或学科中所需的独特能力）。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">So on top of the g-factor, Spearman proposed subject-specific factors, or s-factors.</p>
</details>

例如，学生在数学方面的表现将取决于他们的一般智力加上他们对数学的特定学科因素。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">A student's performance in math, for example, would depend on their general intelligence plus their subject specific factor for math.</p>
</details>

特定学科因素可以提高或降低在该特定科目上的表现。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Subject specific factors could increase or decrease performance on that particular subject.</p>
</details>

斯皮尔曼认为特定因素可以通过训练来提高，但一般智力是固定的。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Spearman believed that specific factors could be trained but general intelligence was fixed.</p>
</details>

因此，他希望找到一种可靠测量一般智力的方法。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">So he wanted to find a way to reliably measure general intelligence.</p>
</details>

### 比奈-西蒙测试与智商的诞生

大约在同一时间，法国的**阿尔弗雷德·比奈**（Alfred Binet: 法国心理学家，智力测试的先驱）受命找出哪些孩子在学校需要更多帮助。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">At around the same time in France, Alfred Binet was tasked with figuring out which kids needed more help in school.</p>
</details>

他与**西奥多·西蒙**（Theodore Simon: 法国心理学家，与比奈共同开发了比奈-西蒙智力测试）合作，开发了**比奈-西蒙测试**（Binet-Simon test: 世界上第一个实用的智力测试，旨在识别需要特殊教育的儿童）。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Together with Theodore Simon, he developed the Benet-Simon test.</p>
</details>

学生们被要求说出图画中缺少的东西、定义抽象术语并重复句子。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Students were asked to name what's missing in the drawing, define abstract terms, and repeat back sentences.</p>
</details>

还有一个问题是问哪张脸最漂亮。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">And there was also this question asking which face is prettiest.</p>
</details>

总共有30项任务。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">There were 30 tasks in all.</p>
</details>

他们的表现与不同年龄的其他学生进行基准比较，以确定他们的**心理年龄**（mental age: 智力测试中，一个人的智力水平相当于普通儿童的平均年龄）。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Their performance was benchmarked against other students of different ages in order to assign them a mental age.</p>
</details>

例如，如果一个学生的表现与平均八岁儿童的表现差不多，那么他们的心理年龄就是八岁。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">For example, if a student performed about as well as the average eight year old, their mental age would be eight.</p>
</details>

然后将这个心理年龄除以他们的实际年龄，再乘以一百，就得到了所谓的智商，智商由此诞生。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">This mental age was then divided by their actual age and multiplied by a hundred to arrive at a so-called intelligence quotient, and IQ was born.</p>
</details>

所以，比奈-西蒙测试是世界上第一个智商测试。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">So the Binet-Simon test was the world's first IQ test.</p>
</details>

它被**亨利·戈达德**（Henry Goddard: 美国心理学家，将比奈-西蒙测试引入美国并推广其应用）翻译成英文并带到美国。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">It was translated by Goddard into English and brought to the U.S.</p>
</details>

在斯坦福大学，**刘易斯·特曼**（Lewis Terman: 美国心理学家，修订了比奈-西蒙测试并将其标准化为斯坦福-比奈测试）使用大量的美国样本对其进行了标准化，并经过一些修改，它成为了**斯坦福-比奈测试**（Stanford-Binet test: 比奈-西蒙测试的美国修订版，是美国使用最广泛的智力测试之一）。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">At Stanford, Lewis Terman standardized it using a large American sample, and with some modifications it became the Stanford-Binet test, and for decades it was the most widely used test in the United States.</p>
</details>

几十年来，它一直是美国使用最广泛的测试。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">and for decades it was the most widely used test in the United States.</p>
</details>

但这仅仅是个开始。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">But this was just the start.</p>
</details>

许多其他智商测试也相继开发出来，它们都有着相同的目标：测量g因子。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Many other IQ tests were developed and they all had the same goal of measuring the g-factor.</p>
</details>

它们通过评估许多不同的心智能力来实现这一点，包括记忆、语言、空间和数字技能。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">The way they did this was by assessing many different mental abilities, including memory, verbal, spatial, and numerical skills.</p>
</details>

这些领域中的每一个都可能存在特定学科的偏差。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Each one of these areas might have a subject-specific shift.</p>
</details>

但通过将它们全部平均起来，其理念是特定学科的影响会相互抵消，从而得到一个对g因子的良好近似值。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</p>
<p class="english-text">But by averaging them all together, the idea was the subject-specific effects would cancel out, leaving a decent approximation of g.</p>
</details>

当然，总会有一些误差。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Of course, there would always be some error.</p>
</details>

但这就是为什么心理学家设计智商测试时，会有七到十个不同的任务部分，以尽量减少特定学科的扭曲。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">But that's why psychologists designed IQ tests with upwards of seven to 10 sections with distinct tasks to try to minimize subject-specific distortions.</p>
</details>

所有不同的智商测试在问题数量和难度上都有所不同。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">All the different IQ tests differed in the number of questions and their difficulty.</p>
</details>

为了标准化评分系统，每个测试都对大量人口样本进行。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">So to standardize the scoring system, each test was given to a large sample of the population.</p>
</details>

原始分数经过标准化处理，通常使平均值为一百，标准差为15，这种做法一直沿用至今。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Raw scores were normalized, usually so the mean was a hundred and the standard deviation was 15, and that's how it's still done to this day.</p>
</details>

这就是智商，它旨在衡量一个人相对于其他人口的g因子。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">This is known as IQ, and it's meant to be a measure of an individual's g-factor in comparison to the rest of the population.</p>
</details>

按照这种量表，68%的人智商在85到115之间。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">The way it's scaled, 68% of people have an IQ between 85 and 115.</p>
</details>

只有大约2%的人得分超过130或低于70。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Only around 2% score over 130 or under 70.</p>
</details>

### 现代智商测试的题型与练习

在我为智商测试做准备时，我练习了现代测试中出现的所有不同类型的问题。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">As I was studying for my IQ test, I practiced all the different types of questions that appear on modern tests.</p>
</details>

其中一个部分几乎肯定是词汇。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">One section will almost certainly be on vocabulary.</p>
</details>

他们会给你一个词，比如“sanguine”（乐观的），你必须从多项选择中选出意义最相似的选项。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">They give you one word like sanguine, and you have to pick which of the multiple choice options is most similar in meaning.</p>
</details>

是“gloomy”（阴郁的）、“asinine”（愚蠢的）、“recalcitrant”（顽抗的）、“optimistic”（乐观的）还是“reflective”（沉思的）？

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Is it gloomy, asinine, recalcitrant, optimistic, or reflective?</p>
</details>

他们也可能会让你选择一个意思相反的词。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">They might also ask you to pick a word with the opposite meaning.</p>
</details>

那么，“perspicacious”（有洞察力的）的反义词是什么？
