### 现代自动扶梯的工程奥秘

这个问题的解决方案来自另一位发明家乔治·惠勒（George Wheeler）。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">The solution to this problem came from another inventor named George Wheeler.</p>

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">His idea forms the basis of every escalator in use today.</p>
</details>

现代版本的工作原理大致如下。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">A modern version of it works something like this.</p>

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">A typical subway escalator has an electric motor at the top with a power output of around 50 kilowatts.</p>

这比大多数电动汽车都要小。

<p class="english-text">Smaller than most electric cars.</p>

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">This motor spins extremely fast at over a 1,000 RPM, but it's pretty weak.</p>
</details>

因此，为了驱动梯级，自动扶梯需要将其转换为较慢但力更大的输出。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>

电机通过一个大链轮连接到一条加固的钢链上，这条链条拉动梯级围绕一个循环运动。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">The motor is connected with a large sprocket to a reinforced steel chain, which pulls the stairs around a loop.</p>
</details>

所谓的梯级链条装有轮子，使其能够平稳地绕过弯道。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>

</details>

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>

</details>

接下来，他在每个梯级上增加了第二组轮子，这些轮子沿着不同的轨道运行，从而可以在任何时候控制每个梯级的角度。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>

</details>
