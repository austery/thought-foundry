### 雪崩的科学原理：雪层、弱层与触发机制

最基本来说，当雪晶之间的晶体键断裂，且将雪固定在山上的摩擦力不足以抵抗重力时，就会发生雪崩。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">At the most basic level, an avalanche happens when the crystalline bonds between the snow crystals break and the force of friction holding the snow on the mountain isn't enough to counteract the pull of gravity.</p>
</details>

雪崩按大小分为一到五级。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Avalanches are categorized by size on a scale of one to five.</p>
</details>

一级雪崩不足以埋住一个人，但五级雪崩将涉及10万立方米的雪，移动数公里，造成大规模破坏。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">One would not be large enough to bury a person, but size five would involve a 100,000 cubic meters of snow traveling, many kilometers causing destruction on a massive scale.</p>
</details>

首先要注意的是，雪并非均匀一致。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">The first thing to note is that snow isn't uniform.</p>
</details>

**雪层**（snowpack: 积雪层，由多层不同性质的雪组成）是一次又一次暴风雪堆积而成的，因此它具有分层结构，这些雪层并非完全相同，并且会随着时间而变化，这取决于它们所暴露的条件——气温、湿度、阳光、风和雨都会影响雪的结构。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">The snow pack is built up one storm at a time, so it's layered and these layers aren't all the same and they change over time, due to the conditions they're exposed to, the air temperature, humidity, sun, wind, and rain all affect the structure of the snow.</p>
</details>

例如，新雪落下后，如果遇到一个温暖晴朗的日子，雪会融化，然后在夜间重新结冰，形成一层冰。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">For example, after fresh snow has fallen, if there's a warm sunny day, the snow will melt and then refreeze overnight creating a sheet of ice.</p>
</details>

最重要的条件是雪层中的**温度梯度**（temperature gradient: 在空间上温度变化的速率），但我们稍后会详细讨论。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">The most important condition is the temperature gradient in the snowpack, but we'll come to that.</p>
</details>

雪层保存着所有发生在其上的事件记录。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">The snowpack preserves a record of everything that has happened to it.</p>
</details>

如果你在雪中挖一个坑，你可以通过阅读不同的雪层来了解它的过去。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">If you dig a pit into the snow, you can read the different layers to understand its past.</p>
</details>

目前公认有九种不同的雪崩问题，但主要有两种类型：**板状雪崩**（slab avalanche: 一整块雪板滑动形成的雪崩）和**松散雪崩**（loose avalanche: 由松散雪粒开始滑动并逐渐扩大规模的雪崩）。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Nine different avalanche problems are recognized, but there are two main types, slab and loose.</p>
</details>

松散雪崩又分为两种：干性松散雪崩和湿性松散雪崩。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">There are two types of loose avalanches, dry loose, and wet loose.</p>
</details>

干性松散雪崩中的雪是松散的，雪粒之间没有完全粘连。因此，一旦被触发，它会呈扇形散开。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">The snow in dry loose avalanches is well, loose. The snow isn't all stuck together. So after it's triggered, it fans out.</p>
</details>

有时松散雪崩也被称为**点释放雪崩**（point release avalanches: 从一个点开始，雪粒逐渐向外扩散的雪崩）。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Sometimes loose avalanches are also known as point release avalanches.</p>
</details>

<details>

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>

<details>

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Honestly, it's kind of fun, it's fun. It's like, it's kind of like surfing where it's dynamic and it's moving and you have to keep moving with the terrain because you know that yeah, the sluff his behind you and if you're not moving fast enough, it's gonna get you.</p>
</details>

另一种雪崩类型是湿性松散雪崩。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Another type of avalanche is wet loose.</p>
</details>

**亲历者:** 它通常发生在春季，在朝南的坡面上。阳光加热积雪，使其变得更密实、泥泞和流动。

<details>

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
