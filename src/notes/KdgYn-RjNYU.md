### 挪威主权财富基金的诞生与增长

我们是多么幸运！1969年，在最后一次钻探中发现了石油。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Oh boy, have we been lucky. Oil was found in one of the last drills in 1969.</p>
</details>

具有前瞻性的政治家们采取行动，确保挪威人民能够从中受益，不仅是我们这一代，也包括未来的世代。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Forward-thinking politicians acted such that the Norwegian people were the ones who benefited, not only our generation but also generations to come.</p>
</details>

1990年，也就是第一笔资金流入前仅仅六年，挪威议会通过了一项法案，成立了当时被称为**挪威政府石油基金**（Norwegian Government Petroleum Fund: 挪威的主权财富基金，后更名为挪威政府全球养老基金）。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">In 1990, just six years prior to the first inflow, the Norwegian parliament adopted an act that established the Norwegian Government Petroleum Fund, as it was called at that time.</p>

所以，时机非常完美；当资金开始流入时，这个组织已经建立起来，准备好进行投资。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">So, the timing was quite excellent; when the money started to flow in, the organization was already set up, ready to invest.</p>
</details>

自那时以来，我们的目标一直是为适度风险争取最高可能的回报。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Since then, our goal has been to maximize, or give the highest possible return for a moderate risk.</p>
</details>

自成立以来，基金已累计增加近50亿**挪威克朗**（kroner: 挪威的货币单位）。

<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Almost 5 billion kroner has been added to the fund since the beginning.</p>

这是从挪威石油和天然气生产收入中节省下来的资金流。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>

回报占基金价值的75%，包括汇率影响。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">The return accounts for 75% of the fund's value, including currency effects.</p>
</details>

这真是令人着迷。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">That is fascinating.</p>
</details>

为了保护挪威本土经济，基金只在挪威境外进行投资。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">To protect the Norwegian mainland economy, the fund is only invested outside of Norway.</p>

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">So, the investments are divided primarily between North America, Europe, and Asia Oceania.</p>

### 资产配置与全球布局

基金的72%投资于**股票**（equities: 代表公司所有权的金融资产），26%投资于**固定收益**（fixed income: 提供固定回报的投资，如债券），2%投资于房地产。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">72% of the fund is invested in equities, 26% in fixed income, and 2% in real estate.</p>
</details>

我们有一条自动规则，当市场上涨时，我们会减持或减少股票，当股票下跌时，我们会买入，以将股票比例保持在70%左右。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">We have an automatic rule that makes us sell down or reduce equities when the market increases, and buy equities when they decline, to keep the equity portion around 70%.</p>
</details>

大多数固定收益资产投资于发达市场，其中很大一部分是欧元、美元、英镑和日元计价的**国债**（treasury bonds: 政府发行的债券）。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Most fixed-income assets are in developed markets, with a large percentage in treasury bonds issued in euros, dollars, pounds, and yen.</p>
</details>

我们还在全球多个城市的近900处房产进行投资，包括伦敦、巴黎、柏林、纽约、波士顿、旧金山和东京的办公楼、零售和物流地产。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">We also invest in close to 900 properties in several global cities, including offices, retail, and logistics sectors in London, Paris, Berlin, New York, Boston, San Francisco, and Tokyo.</p>
</details>

基金还投资了大约9,000家公司，遍布71个国家，全部是上市股票。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">The fund is also invested in almost 9,000 companies across, I think, 71 countries, all listed equities.</p>
</details>

平均而言，基金拥有所有上市公司1.5%的股份，这使其成为全球最大的单一公司所有者之一，这同样令人着迷。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">On average, the fund owns 1.5% of all listed companies, making it one of the world's largest single owners of companies, which is quite fascinating.</p>
</details>

股票管理基于两大策略：市场敞口和基于基本面研究的证券选择。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Equity management is based on two main strategies: market exposure and security selection based on fundamental research.</p>
</details>

这些策略使我们能够获得股票基准所体现的广泛股票市场敞口，同时通过公司分析和证券选择提高回报。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">These strategies enable us to achieve exposure to the broad equity market, as expressed in the equity benchmark, while simultaneously increasing returns based on company analysis and security selection.</p>
</details>

### 外部管理人：在地专业知识与超额收益

在伦敦、纽约和新加坡，我们组织全球行业团队，专注于亚洲的新兴市场和小型股公司。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">In London, New York, and Singapore, we organize global sector teams focusing on emerging markets and small-cap companies in Asia.</p>
</details>

所有这些团队都以国家为中心，并设在各自的国家，例如，专注于印度、巴西、日本小型股和韩国小型股。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">All of them are country-focused and based in the respective countries, for example, focusing on India, Brazil, Japan small-cap, and Korea small-cap.</p>
</details>

在欧洲和美国，情况则更为复杂，既有本地的、专注于国家的、小型管理人，也有一些区域和行业管理人。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">In Europe and the US, it's a bit more mixed, with both locally based, country-focused small managers and some regional and sector managers.</p>
</details>

关键在于我们寻找对少数领域非常了解的专家。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">The key is that we look for specialists who know a few areas very well.</p>
</details>

如今，我们在全球50个不同城市拥有110名管理人。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Today, we have 110 managers in 50 different cities across the world.</p>
</details>

我们坚信身处当地的信息优势。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">We strongly believe in the information advantage of being on the ground.</p>
</details>

我们寻求建立基于他们广泛的本地知识和研究的投资组合。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">We seek to build portfolios grounded in their extensive local knowledge and research.</p>
</details>

他们通过频繁的现场访问而非仅仅参加会议，与投资组合中的公司保持定期沟通。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">They maintain regular communication with the companies in the portfolios by frequently visiting them on-site, rather than just attending conferences.</p>
</details>

我们的目标是产生**超额收益**（excess return: 超过基准指数或同类投资平均水平的收益）。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Our aim is to generate excess return.</p>
</details>

截至去年，自成立以来，我们已产生了1.8%的年化超额收益（扣除费用后），总计900亿克朗。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">As of last year, we have generated an annualized excess return of 1.8% after fees since the beginning, which amounts to 90 billion kroner.</p>
</details>

随着时间的推移，我们只支付了3%的费用，这意味着80%的超额收益由我们保留。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Over time, we have only paid 3% in fees, meaning 80% of the excess return is kept by us.</p>
</details>

我们还认为，外部管理人通过避免投资那些在这些市场中具有问题商业模式和薄弱公司治理的公司，从而降低了过度投资的风险。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">We also believe that external managers reduce the risk of over-investments by avoiding certain companies with problematic business models and weak corporate governance in these markets.</p>
</details>

这些管理人通常在风险敞口较高的市场中运作。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">These managers operate in markets with higher exposure risk.</p>
</details>

我们对**ESG**（Environmental, Social, and Governance: 环境、社会和公司治理）问题的关注已融入到选择和监控过程中。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Our focus on ESG issues is integrated into the selection and monitoring process.</p>
</details>

例如，在选择管理人后，我们会向他们发送期望文件并讨论ESG问题，以确保他们理解对我们而言重要的事情，并在代表我们投资时牢记在心。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">For example, after selecting a manager, we send expectation documents to them and discuss ESG issues to ensure they understand what is important to us and keep it in mind when investing on our behalf.</p>

事实上，本地管理人避免了其本地基准中75%的公司。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Local managers have actually avoided 75% of the companies in their local benchmarks.</p>
</details>

我们的策略随着基金规模的扩大、市场环境的变化而不断演变。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Our strategy has evolved over time with the expansion of the fund's universe, its size, and changes in the market environment.</p>
</details>

我们的方法始终是动态的，我们选择关注的细分市场也发生了变化，例如2000年专注于小型股，2005年专注于区域管理人。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Our approach has always been dynamic, and the segments we choose to focus on have changed, such as a small-cap focus in 2000, and regional managers in 2005.</p>

这涉及到大量的通过额外研究可能更好地评估的不确定性。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">This involves a great deal of uncertainty that may be better assessed through additional research.</p>
</details>

我们预计未来变化将继续，因为我们知道机会会发生变化。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">We expect changes to continue going forward, as we know there will be changes in opportunities.</p>
</details>

不变的是，我们所有的委托都基于深入的基本面研究，并且都采用独立账户。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">What has not changed is that all our mandates are based on deep fundamental research, and they are all on segregated accounts.</p>
</details>

这意味着他们以我们的名义在我们的账簿上进行交易。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">That means they are trading on our books in our name.</p>

我们这样做有几个原因。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">There are several reasons why we do that.</p>
</details>

首先，我们随时可以看到他们买卖了什么，因此我们了解每个投资组合的风险、持仓、变化以及它们的组合。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>

### 管理人选择哲学与流程

你可能已经看过电影**《点球成金》**（Moneyball: 讲述一支棒球队如何通过数据分析而非高薪来组建有竞争力的队伍），布拉德·皮特饰演的**比利·比恩**（Billy Beane）为奥克兰运动家队组建了一支棒球队，因为他们无法在薪资上竞争。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">You may have seen the movie Moneyball, with Brad Pitt as Billy Beane, who builds a team of baseball players for the Oakland A's because they can't compete on salary.</p>
</details>

我们遵循类似的过程。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">We follow a similar process.</p>
</details>

我们不根据管理人过去的表现来决定是否聘用他们，因为我们相信预期的表现会很好，而不是他们过去表现好。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">We don't look at hiring managers based on their past performance because we believe expected performance will be good, not necessarily that they have had good performance historically.</p>
</details>

历史表现可能会隐藏某些因素，例如，良好的表现是否是由团队中曾工作过的某些分析师所驱动。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Historical performance may hide certain factors, for example, whether good performance was driven by certain analysts who used to work in the team.</p>
</details>

此外，我们需要很长的历史数据来判断表现是否由技能驱动，而我们常常没有这样的历史数据。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Furthermore, we need a long history to figure out if performance is driven by skill, and very frequently, we don't have that history.</p>
</details>

其次，决定哪个管理人应该获得委托的不是投资委员会，而是一个独立的投资组合管理人。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Second, it's not an investment committee that decides which manager should be awarded a mandate, but an individual portfolio manager.</p>
</details>

我们曾说过，聘用外部管理人是一项投资决策，而投资决策不应在集体环境中做出。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">We have said that employing external managers is an investment decision, and investment decisions should not be made in group settings.</p>
</details>

因此，我们团队中有八人负责选择管理人，我们团队中的每个人都是负责选择和终止的投资组合管理人。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">So, we have eight people in the team selecting managers, and each of us is a portfolio manager responsible for selection and termination.</p>
</details>

在每个委托中，都有一位专门的决策者。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Within each, there is one dedicated decision-maker for the mandate.</p>
</details>

我们不像许多其他客户那样使用顾问来协助我们。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">We don't use consultants to assist us, unlike many other clients.</p>
</details>

我们决定深入挖掘，了解管理人投资组合理念和团队互动的各个方面，因为我们正在努力创建由不同投资组合管理人组成的投资组合，这些管理人具有不同的重点和投资范围，并能很好地协同工作。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">We have decided to delve deep to know every aspect of the manager's portfolio philosophy and team interaction because we are striving to create a portfolio of different portfolio managers that fit well together, with different focuses and investment universes.</p>
</details>

那么，我们具体怎么做呢？我们遵循一个相当结构化的选择过程。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">So, what do we do? We follow a quite structured selection process.</p>
</details>

它始于向潜在管理人发送一份简短问卷，询问他们的组织、团队、完整投资组合和主要竞争对手。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">It starts with a short questionnaire to potential managers, asking about their organization, teams, full portfolio, and main competitors.</p>
</details>

这些信息有助于我们更好地了解其他管理人。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">This information helps us gain better insight into additional managers.</p>
</details>

在此基础上，我们拥有一个庞大的潜在管理人数据集，可以对他们的实际投资组合、购买变化以及彼此之间的差异进行不同的分析。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Based on that, we have a huge dataset of potential managers and can perform different analyses on their actual portfolios, changes in what they've been buying, and differences between each.</p>
</details>

这会形成一个候选名单，我们可以与他们在现场会面。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">This leads to a shortlist, and we can meet them on-site.</p>
</details>

那些被选中进入第二轮和第三轮的管理人将接受更广泛的运营尽职调查，在选择前与我们的团队会面三到五次。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Those selected for the second and third rounds undergo more extensive operational due diligence, meeting our team three to five times before selection.</p>
</details>

在这些会议中，有几位管理人会被淘汰，同时也会发现新的管理人并将其纳入搜索范围。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Several are screened out during these meetings, while new managers are found and included in the search.</p>
</details>

每次会议，我们都会对投资组合的变化和团队的潜在变化进行更多分析。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Each time we have these meetings, we conduct a lot more analysis on portfolio changes and potential team changes.</p>
</details>

这个过程是持续重复的。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">This process is repeated continuously.</p>
</details>

对于所有我们合作的潜在管理人，我们通常在选择前与他们会面三到四次，并在选择后每季度会面一次。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">For all potential managers we invest with, we usually meet them three to four times before selection and once per quarter after they are selected.</p>
</details>

在这些会议中，我们讨论投资组合、个别公司、他们何时会见了公司管理层、他们谈论了什么、讨论了哪些ESG问题、公司的定价情况，以及他们为何与市场其他参与者持有不同观点。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">In these meetings, we discuss the portfolio, individual companies, when they met the management of those companies, what they talked about, which ESG issues they discussed, what is priced into the company, and why they have a different view from the rest of the market.</p>
</details>

这包括他们已投资的公司和他们决定不投资的公司。
<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
