<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">What now around 300 BC Euclid was actually thinking along similar lines when he discovered the pattern that makes these perfect numbers.</p>

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Take the number one and double it, you get two now, keep doubling it.</p>

<p class="english-text">You get 4, 8, 16, 32, 64, and so on.</p>
</details>

<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>

</details>
这是素数。
<details>

<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">So you multiply it by 16 and you get 496.</p>

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">The third perfect number.</p>
