### 拥抱问题：从抵触到解脱

我的名字是奥利弗·伯克曼（Oliver Burkeman），我是一名作家，也是一位正在戒除**效率狂人**（Productivity Geek: 对效率极度痴迷的人）习惯的人，我写了《凡人的冥想：拥抱局限，为重要之事腾出时间的四周指南》（"Meditations for Mortals: Four Weeks to Embrace Your Limitations and Make Time for What Counts"）这本书。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">My name is Oliver Burkeman. I'm an author and a recovering productivity geek, and I wrote the book "Meditations for Mortals: Four Weeks to Embrace Your Limitations and Make Time for What Counts."</p>
</details>

我认为，当谈到问题——那些充斥我们生活、普通而烦人的问题时——我们常常让事情变得更糟，因为我们对它们有一种双重抵触。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">I think when it comes to problems — just, you know, the ordinary, annoying problems that fill so much of life — we make things a lot worse for ourselves, because we have a sort of dual objection to them, right?</p>
</details>

一方面，当你处理一个问题时，无论问题是什么，它都是你需要努力解决的。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">So on the one hand, when you're dealing with a problem, there's whatever the problem is, and it's it's something you've got to grapple with.</p>
</details>

但很多时候，我们内心深处还有一种感觉，认为我们根本不应该面对问题。我们以为到了现在这个人生阶段，就不必再处理这些问题了。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">But there's also this background sense a lot of the time that we somehow shouldn't be facing problems at all. We thought we would have got to the stage in our lives by now where we didn't have to deal with problems.</p>
</details>

我们觉得，如果不是这些问题，我们就能把工作做得很好；如果不是这些问题，家庭生活就会非常美好。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">That we could do our jobs really well if only there weren't all these problems, or that family life would be so great if only we didn't have to deal with these problems.</p>
</details>

所以，我们实际上是在让事情变得更糟，因为我们不仅要处理问题本身，还要处理这种对生活中竟然存在问题的愤慨。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">So we actually make it worse for ourselves because we're dealing both with the problem and with this sort of indignation that there should even be problems in our lives.</p>
</details>

### 问题即意义：生活的共鸣

我认为，如果你能培养出一种“品味”生活中的问题的能力，那会非常有帮助。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">I think it really helps if you can develop a taste for having problems in life.</p>
</details>

我的意思是，首先要认识到，这种内心深处想要达到“没有问题”状态的要求，从表面上看是荒谬的。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">And what I mean by that is to start, really, just with the recognition that this inner demand to get to the place with no problems is sort of absurd on its face.</p>
</details>

当然，有些特定的问题是我们希望避免的，是绝不希望任何人遇到的。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">There are definitely specific problems that one would hope to avoid, that one would never wish on anybody.</p>
</details>

但如果你从最普遍的层面思考问题是什么，它就像是你的有限能力在某种程度上与现实发生冲突的某个领域。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">But if you think about what a problem is at the most general level, it's just like, some area where your limited capacities are running up against reality in some way.</p>
</details>

结果就是，有些事情你必须去处理。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">And as a result, there's something you have to address yourself to.</p>

换句话说，那就是“生活中富有意义的活动”。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Like, another word for that or another phrase for that is "meaningful activity in life."</p>

我认为，一个没有任何问题的生活，将是一个失去了**共鸣**（Resonance: 德国社会理论家**哈特穆特·罗萨**提出的概念，指人与世界之间有意义的互动和连接）的生活，正如德国社会理论家哈特穆特·罗萨（Hartmut Rosa）所说。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>

这种没有问题的生活会显得非常空虚和毫无意义。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">There would be something very, very empty and meaningless about this sort of life with no problems.</p>
</details>

我的一位朋友描述了她顿悟的时刻，她意识到自己花了很多时间思考，如果不必处理所有这些问题，她的工作会做得多好。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>

</details>

然后她意识到，实际上，从一个非常深刻的意义上讲，这些问题就是她的工作。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">And then realizing that actually in a very profound sense, the problems were the job.</p>
</details>

她之所以能胜任这个职位，正是因为她有创造性和精力去处理意想不到的事情并找到解决方案。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">The reason she was in that position was her creative and energetic ability to deal with unforeseen things and find resolutions to them.</p>
</details>

此外，如果你的工作没有任何这些创造性的挑战，如果它能完全简化为一套绝对可预测的步骤，那将毫无乐趣。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">And apart from anything else, you know, if your job didn't feature any of those creative challenges, if it could be completely reduced to an absolutely predictable set of steps, it wouldn't be any fun.</p>
</details>

它也极易被完全自动化。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">It would also be extremely prey to being automated away entirely.</p>

### 避免的代价：啃噬之鼠

阻碍我们去做那些我们认为想做的事情，过上有意义的生活和工作的因素之一，就是逃避。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">So one of the things that absolutely gets in the way of our just doing the things we think we want to do with our lives and living and working meaningfully is avoidance.</p>
</details>

这是一种感觉，觉得生活中有某些任务或领域会引发如此多的恐惧或威慑，以至于我们根本不去触碰它们。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">It's the sense that there are certain tasks or areas of life that trigger so much kind of fear or intimidation that we just sort of don't go there.</p>
</details>

你担心银行账户里钱不够，所以你甚至不去查余额；或者你担心某种身体疼痛可能是严重疾病的征兆，所以你也不去检查。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">You're worried you might not have enough money in your checking account, so you don't even check the balance, or you're worried that some physical pain could be the sign of something serious, so you don't get it looked into.</p>
</details>

这是一种非常普遍且可以理解的人类行为，就是不去行动。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Very common and sort of very understandable human behavior to just not do things.</p>
</details>

你只是忽略车库，因为里面有太多杂物，你无法忍受清理它的想法。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">You just ignore the garage because there's just too much junk in there, and you can't bear the thought of trying to clear it out.</p>
</details>

一位名叫**保罗·卢曼斯**（Paul Loomans: 荷兰禅宗僧侣）的荷兰禅宗僧侣曾非常雄辩地论述过这种现象。他将我们一直逃避的这些事情称为**“啃噬之鼠”**（Gnawing Rats: 保罗·卢曼斯提出的概念，指那些被我们逃避、在夜深人静时会困扰我们的烦恼或任务）。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">There's a Dutch Zen monk called Paul Loomans who's written very eloquently on this phenomenon and he talks about these things that we avoid all the time as "gnawing rats" that kind of, you know, in the middle of the night, you'll remember them and you'll feel bad about them.</p>
</details>

它们会在半夜啃噬着你，让你感到不安，但基本上，你一生都在试图避开它们。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">They'll be gnawing at you, but basically, you spend your life just sort of trying to get out of their way.</p>
</details>

他建议我们实际上需要做的是与这些“啃噬之鼠”成为朋友，这是采取行动解决它们的途径。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">And he suggests that what we need to do is actually to sort of befriend our gnawing rats, the path towards doing something about them.</p>
</details>

在他的比喻中，这意味着将它们从“啃噬之鼠”转化为温顺的白色绵羊，它们非常听话，不会造成同样的问题。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">And in his imagery, transforming them from gnawing rats into fluffy white sheep that just sort of very docile and don't cause the same problems.</p>
</details>

### 驯服“啃噬之鼠”：微小行动的力量

关键的一步是转向我们的“啃噬之鼠”。我们需要与它们成为朋友。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">The key step is to just sort of turn towards our gnawing rats. We need to befriend them.</p>
</details>

换句话说，你只需要做一些事情，有意识地与这项任务建立心理上的关系。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">In other words, you just need to do something to consciously build a psychological relationship with that task.</p>

所以，你可能实际上什么都不用做。

<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">So it might not actually be that you do anything about it.</p>
</details>

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">If you've got a whole, like, shed full of junk that needs to be sorted out and you can't bear to go in to to deal with it, it might just be a question of going into that space.</p>

<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>

任何类似的事情都会将那个项目、任务或生活领域带回到你所承认的现实中。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Anything like that will have the effect of bringing that project or task or domain of life back into your sort of acknowledged reality.</p>
</details>

然后，正如卢曼斯所说——这也是我的经验——在那之后不久，采取这项活动的第一步的时机可能就会到来。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>

但必须有一个有意识的过程，去把它带回来，与它建立关系，而不是假装——不是投入所有精力去假装——它不存在，也不是你生活的一部分，尽管它已经是。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">But there's got to be this conscious process of kind of bringing it back, having a relationship with it, not pretending — not putting all this energy into pretending — that it doesn't exist and isn't a part of your life when it when it already is.</p>

### 拥抱不适与“尴尬”的建议

我认为，当涉及到推进那些因为新颖和代表某种成长、挑战你内心深处的事物时，有两件事需要记住。

<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>

<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>

如果你正在做出的某种改变让你感到不适，那正是它正在深刻挑战你内心深处固有事物的迹象，你应该预料到它会让你感到尴尬。

<details>
