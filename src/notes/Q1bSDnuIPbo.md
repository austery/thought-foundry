### 银河系中心黑洞的首张图像

这是一个位于我们银河系中心、被称为**人马座A***（Sagittarius A*: 银河系中心的超大质量黑洞）的超大质量黑洞的图像。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">This is a picture of the supermassive black hole at the center of our Milky Way galaxy known as Sagittarius A*.</p>
</details>

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">The black hole itself doesn't emit light so what we're seeing is the hot plasma swirling around it.</p>
</details>

这是有史以来拍摄到的第二张黑洞图像。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>

</details>

这张图像是由**事件视界望远镜**（Event Horizon Telescope: EHT，一个全球射电望远镜网络）合作组织拍摄的，他们也曾发布了位于M87星系中心的超大质量黑洞图像。

<details>

<details>

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">But the biggest challenge of all in making an image of either supermassive black hole is that these objects are so compact and so far from Earth, in the sky, they appear very, very tiny.</p>

### 甚长基线干涉测量（VLBI）的原理

首先，这些黑洞图像并非用可见光拍摄。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">So starting with, how did they make these images of black holes? Well, the first thing to know is they weren't made with visible light.</p>
</details>

它们是使用波长为1.3毫米的无线电波拍摄的。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">They were made using radio waves with a wavelength of 1.3 millimeters.</p>
</details>

因此，所有观测都是由射电望远镜进行的，它们看起来基本上就像巨大的卫星天线。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">So all the observations were taken by radio telescopes which essentially look like huge satellite dishes.</p>
</details>

当一个源发出无线电波时，它们会向四面八方辐射出去，但地球距离太远，以至于当它们到达我们星球时，波前几乎完全是平坦且平行的。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">When a source emits radio waves, they travel out radially in all directions, but Earth is so far away that by the time they reach our planet, the wavefronts are almost completely flat and parallel.</p>
</details>

这被称为平面波。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">This is known as a plane wave.</p>
</details>

射电望远镜通过在天空中来回扫描来工作。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">A radio telescope works by scanning back and forth across the sky.</p>
</details>

当它直接指向一个无线电源时，会产生一个亮点。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">When it is pointed directly at a radio source, it produces a bright spot.</p>
</details>

这是因为所有无线电波传播相同的距离，从天线反射回来，并同时被接收，所以它们是同相的，这意味着波峰与波峰对齐，波谷与波谷对齐。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">That's because all the radio waves travel the same distance, bounce off the dish, and are received at the same time so they are in phase meaning peaks line up with peaks and troughs with troughs.</p>
</details>

它们发生建设性干涉。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">They constructively interfere.</p>
</details>

当望远镜移过光源时，一些无线电波现在传播的距离比其他波更远，因此它们会异相相遇，发生破坏性干涉，信号强度降至零。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">As the telescope moves past the source, some of the radio waves now travel farther than others and therefore they meet up out of phase, destructively interfere, and the intensity of the signal drops to zero.</p>
</details>

为了制作清晰的图像，你希望这种下降尽可能陡峭，这样望远镜只有在直接对准光源时才能产生峰值强度，然后当望远镜稍微向任何方向移动时，强度就会迅速下降。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">To make a sharp image, you want this drop off to be as steep as possible so the telescope produces peak intensity only when aimed directly at the source and then the intensity drops rapidly when the dish is moved just a tiny bit in any direction.</p>
</details>

有两种方法可以实现这一点。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">There are two ways of achieving this.</p>
</details>

一种是观测更高频率的无线电波。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">One is to observe higher frequency radio waves.</p>
</details>

这样，望远镜的任何微小移动都代表了波长更大的一个部分。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">That way any slight movement of the telescope represents a greater fraction of a wavelength.</p>
</details>

这会导致破坏性干涉更快发生。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">This causes destructive interference to occur sooner.</p>
</details>

另一种方法是增加望远镜的直径，这会增加望远镜相对两侧无线电波的路径长度差异，以实现给定的角度调整。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">The other way is to increase the diameter of the telescope, and this increases the difference in path length between radio waves on opposite sides of the telescope for a given angular adjustment.</p>
</details>

望远镜识别无线电源的精确程度被称为其**角分辨率**（angular resolution: 望远镜区分两个相邻物体细节的能力）。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">How narrowly a telescope can identify the source of radio waves is known as its angular resolution.</p>
</details>

你可以将其视为望远镜敏感的天空中光斑的大小。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">You can think of it as the size of the spot on the sky that the telescope is sensitive to.</p>
</details>

它与波长成正比，与望远镜直径成反比。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">It is proportional to wavelength, and inversely proportional to the diameter of the telescope.</p>
</details>

拍摄黑洞图像的挑战在于，你试图在天空中一个微小区域内看到其结构。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">The challenge with making a picture of a black hole is that you're trying to see the structure in a tiny area of the sky.</p>
</details>

想象一下用射电望远镜扫描黑洞中心。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Imagine scanning a radio telescope across the center of a black hole.</p>
</details>

你希望当望远镜经过左边缘时看到亮点，然后是暗点，然后当它经过右边缘时再看到另一个亮点。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">You would want to see the bright spot as the telescope passes over the left edge and then a dark spot and then another bright spot as it passes the right edge.</p>
</details>

问题是，对于地球上的任何单个射电望远镜来说，角分辨率都太大了。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">The problem is, for any individual radio telescope on Earth, the angular resolution is too large.</p>
</details>

所以当它经过黑洞时，它在开始接收右侧无线电波的同时，仍然会接收到左侧的无线电波。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">So as it passes over the black hole, it would still be receiving radio waves from the left side as it begins receiving radio waves from the right side.</p>
</details>

分辨率不够高，无法判断那里是否有我们预期的黑洞环形结构，或者它只是一个模糊的斑点。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">The resolution isn't high enough to tell if there's a ring structure there as we'd expect with a black hole, or if it's just a blob.</p>
</details>

观测更短波长的光线实际上不是一个选择，因为那种光会被我们的大气层或黑洞周围的物质阻挡。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Observing at shorter wavelengths isn't really an option because that light is blocked either by our atmosphere or by the matter around the black hole.</p>
</details>

所以如果你想提高分辨率，唯一的方法就是增加望远镜的直径。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">So if you wanna improve resolution, the only way you can do it is by increasing the diameter of the telescope.</p>
</details>

但如果你真的进行计算，你会发现你需要一个地球大小的望远镜才能看到黑洞的环，这显然是不可能的。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">But if you actually do the calculation, you find that the telescope you'd need would have to be the size of the Earth in order to see the ring of a black hole, which is obviously impossible.</p>
</details>

但有一种方法可以做到几乎同样好。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">but there is a way to do something that's almost as good.</p>
</details>

你不需要一个地球大小的完整天线，只需要它的碎片。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">You don't need a complete dish the size of the Earth, just pieces of it.</p>
</details>

这些独立的射电望远镜之间相距的距离可达地球直径。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">Individual radio telescopes that are separated by distances up to the Earth's diameter.</p>
</details>

只要你能正确地组合来自所有这些遥远望远镜的信号，你就能获得所需的建设性干涉和破坏性干涉，从而达到与地球大小天线相同的角分辨率。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">As long as you can properly combine the signals from all these distant telescopes, you get the constructive and destructive interference required to achieve the same angular resolution as an Earth-sized dish.</p>
</details>

这种技术被称为**甚长基线干涉测量**（Very Long Baseline Interferometry: VLBI，一种利用多个相距遥远的望远镜组合观测以提高分辨率的技术）。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">This technique is called very long baseline interferometry.</p>
</details>

因此，事件视界望远镜不仅仅是一个望远镜，而是一个全球射电观测站网络。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">So the event horizon telescope is not just one telescope but a global network of radio observatories.</p>
</details>

所有这些望远镜同时观测人马座A*。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
<p class="english-text">All these telescopes observe Sagittarius A* at the same time.</p>
</details>

与单个望远镜不同，你不能将所有无线电波反射到一个中央接收器并实时叠加它们。

<details>
<summary>View/Hide Original English</summary>
